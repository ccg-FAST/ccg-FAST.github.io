Survey Overview

The M31 Survey is a comprehensive HI mapping project targeting the Andromeda Galaxy (Messier 31) and its surroundings using the FAST telescope. As our nearest major spiral galaxy neighbor, M31 provides an unparalleled laboratory for studying the detailed distribution and kinematics of neutral hydrogen in a large galaxy system.

Why M31?

The Andromeda Galaxy offers unique advantages for detailed HI studies:

Proximity

At only ~780 kpc, M31 is close enough to resolve individual HI features while being representative of large spiral galaxies.

üåå

Size

With a diameter of ~220 kpc in HI, M31 is one of the largest galaxies in the local universe, offering rich structure to study.

Interactions

M31 has experienced interactions with its satellite galaxies (M32, M110), leaving signatures in the HI distribution.

üìä

Comparison

As a twin to the Milky Way, M31 allows us to study a similar galaxy from an external perspective.

Key Science Goals

  • HI Distribution: Map the detailed distribution of neutral hydrogen across M31's disk and halo
  • Kinematics: Measure rotation curves and velocity fields to constrain the mass distribution
  • Warps and Lopsidedness: Study the warped HI disk and its connection to satellite interactions
  • High-Velocity Clouds: Detect and characterize HI clouds in the halo and their accretion history
  • Streams and Tails: Search for tidal features from past galaxy interactions
  • Dark Matter: Use HI kinematics to probe the dark matter halo profile

Previous HI Studies of M31

M31 has been the target of numerous HI studies over the decades:

Survey Telescope Resolution Coverage
Roberts (1966) Green Bank 300-ft ~10 arcmin Central regions
Newton & Emerson (1977) Cambridge ~16 arcmin Full disk
Brinks & Shane (1984) Westerbork ~1 arcmin Selected regions
Chellouche et al. (2009) GMRT ~2 arcmin Halo clouds
FAST M31 Survey FAST ~3 arcmin Full disk + halo

The FAST M31 Survey complements these previous studies with unprecedented sensitivity to faint, extended HI emission.
