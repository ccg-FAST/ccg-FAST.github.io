    <section class="content-section">
        <h2 class="section-title">Survey Overview</h2>
        
        <p>The <strong>M31 Survey</strong> is a comprehensive HI mapping project targeting the Andromeda Galaxy (Messier 31) and its surroundings using the FAST telescope. As our nearest major spiral galaxy neighbor, M31 provides an unparalleled laboratory for studying the detailed distribution and kinematics of neutral hydrogen in a large galaxy system.</p>
        
        <div class="info-box">
            <h3 class="info-box-title">Survey Highlights</h3>

                <li><strong>Distance:</strong> ~780 kpc</li>
                <li><strong>HI mass:</strong> ~5 √ó 10‚Åπ M‚òâ</li>

        <h2 class="section-title">Why M31?</h2>
        
        <p>The Andromeda Galaxy offers unique advantages for detailed HI studies:</p>

            <div class="card">

                    <h3 class="card-title">Proximity</h3>
                </div>
                <div class="card-body">
                    <p class="card-text">At only ~780 kpc, M31 is close enough to resolve individual HI features while being representative of large spiral galaxies.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
            
            <div class="card">
                <div class="card-header">

                    <p class="card-text">With a diameter of ~220 kpc in HI, M31 is one of the largest galaxies in the local universe, offering rich structure to study.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
            
            <div class="card">

    <!-- Science Goals -->
    <section class="content-section">
        <h2 class="section-title">Key Science Goals</h2>
        
        <ul>
            <li><strong>HI Distribution:</strong> Map the detailed distribution of neutral hydrogen across M31's disk and halo</li>
            <li><strong>Kinematics:</strong> Measure rotation curves and velocity fields to constrain the mass distribution</li>
            <li><strong>Warps and Lopsidedness:</strong> Study the warped HI disk and its connection to satellite interactions</li>
            <li><strong>High-Velocity Clouds:</strong> Detect and characterize HI clouds in the halo and their accretion history</li>
            <li><strong>Streams and Tails:</strong> Search for tidal features from past galaxy interactions</li>
            <li><strong>Dark Matter:</strong> Use HI kinematics to probe the dark matter halo profile</li>
        </ul>
    </section>
