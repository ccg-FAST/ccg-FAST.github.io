Primary Science Goals

The M31 survey is designed to address fundamental questions about the structure, kinematics, and evolution of our nearest major spiral galaxy neighbor:

1. HI Distribution and Morphology

M31 exhibits a complex HI distribution that extends well beyond its optical disk. Our survey will:

  • Map the full extent of the HI disk, including faint outer regions
  • Characterize the warped structure of the HI disk
  • Study the lopsidedness and its physical origin
  • Detect faint HI in the halo and circumgalactic medium
  • Compare with stellar and other gas components

2. Rotation Curve and Mass Distribution

The HI kinematics provide crucial constraints on the mass distribution:

  • Measure the rotation curve to large radii (>30 kpc)
  • Constrain the dark matter halo profile
  • Test for dark matter disk or other non-standard components
  • Compare with dynamical mass models
  • Study the Tully-Fisher relation for M31

3. High-Velocity Clouds and Accretion

M31's halo contains numerous HI clouds that may represent ongoing accretion:

  • Compile a complete catalog of high-velocity clouds (HVCs)
  • Study the spatial and velocity distribution of HVCs
  • Measure cloud properties (mass, size, velocity dispersion)
  • Investigate connections to satellite galaxies and streams
  • Estimate the current accretion rate of gas onto M31

Multi-wavelength Context

The M31 survey benefits from extensive multi-wavelength coverage:

  • Optical: Pan-STARRS, SDSS, and other surveys for stellar distribution
  • Infrared: Spitzer, WISE, and Herschel data for dust and stellar mass
  • UV: GALEX for recent star formation
  • X-ray: Chandra and XMM for hot gas and X-ray binaries
  • Radio continuum: Synchrotron emission and thermal emission
