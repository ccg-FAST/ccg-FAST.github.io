        <h2 class="section-title">Primary Science Goals</h2>
        
        <p>The FAST-M31 survey addresses fundamental questions about gas structure, satellite interactions, and cosmological context — spanning from M31's inner disk to its 160 kpc halo and beyond to background galaxies at z &lt; 0.09:</p>
        
        <h3 class="section-subtitle">1. Satellite Galaxies: HI Gas and Tidal Interactions</h3>
        <p>Deep FAST observations of M31's satellite system reveal diffuse HI structures tracing the history of gravitational interactions within the halo, including tidal streams, stripped gas, and circumgalactic HI around dwarf satellites.</p>

        <h3 class="section-subtitle">2. Galactic Foreground: SNRs and Large-Scale Ionized Structures</h3>
        <p>The survey field covers foreground Milky Way structures, enabling parallel science on HI clouds associated with supernova remnants and large-scale ionized structures, constraining SNR-driven energy injection into the ISM.</p>

        <h3 class="section-subtitle">3. High-Velocity Clouds: Distribution, Origin, and Dynamics</h3>
        <p>A systematic census of HI clouds across the full 700 deg² field catalogs the mass, velocity, and spatial distribution of high-velocity clouds within M31's halo, distinguishing populations by origin — tidal debris, Magellanic Stream infall, or M33 interaction fragments.</p>

        <h3 class="section-subtitle">4. M31–M33 Interaction History</h3>
        <p>By searching for HI bridges and tidal debris between M31 and M33, the survey provides dynamical constraints on the past encounter and future evolution of the two largest Local Group members.</p>

        <h3 class="section-subtitle">5. Outer Disk Structure and UV Background Constraints</h3>
        <p>The deep radial HI profile of M31's outer disk is used to constrain the local UV background ionization rate, connecting resolved galaxy observations to the ionization state of the intergalactic medium.</p>

        <h3 class="section-subtitle">6. Background HI Galaxy Survey</h3>
        <p>The deep 700 deg² dataset doubles as one of the most sensitive blind HI surveys of the northern sky, with ~10,000 background galaxy detections enabling studies of the HI mass function, cosmic HI density, and large-scale structure in the Local Volume.</p>
