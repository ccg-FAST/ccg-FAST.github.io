    <section class="content-section">
        <h2 class="section-title">Primary Science Goals</h2>
        
        <p>The M31 survey is designed to address fundamental questions about the structure, kinematics, and evolution of our nearest major spiral galaxy neighbor:</p>
        
        <h3 class="section-subtitle">1. HI Distribution and Morphology</h3>
        <p>M31 exhibits a complex HI distribution that extends well beyond its optical disk. Our survey will:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Map the full extent of the HI disk, including faint outer regions</li>
            <li>Characterize the warped structure of the HI disk</li>
            <li>Study the lopsidedness and its physical origin</li>
            <li>Detect faint HI in the halo and circumgalactic medium</li>
            <li>Compare with stellar and other gas components</li>
        </ul>
        
        <h3 class="section-subtitle">2. Rotation Curve and Mass Distribution</h3>
        <p>The HI kinematics provide crucial constraints on the mass distribution:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Measure the rotation curve to large radii (>30 kpc)</li>
            <li>Constrain the dark matter halo profile</li>
            <li>Test for dark matter disk or other non-standard components</li>
            <li>Compare with dynamical mass models</li>
            <li>Study the Tully-Fisher relation for M31</li>
        </ul>
        
        <h3 class="section-subtitle">3. High-Velocity Clouds and Accretion</h3>
        <p>M31's halo contains numerous HI clouds that may represent ongoing accretion:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Compile a complete catalog of high-velocity clouds (HVCs)</li>
            <li>Study the spatial and velocity distribution of HVCs</li>
            <li>Measure cloud properties (mass, size, velocity dispersion)</li>
            <li>Investigate connections to satellite galaxies and streams</li>
            <li>Estimate the current accretion rate of gas onto M31</li>
        </ul>
    </section>

    <!-- Multi-wavelength -->
    <section class="content-section">
        <h2 class="section-title">Multi-wavelength Context</h2>
        
        <p>The M31 survey benefits from extensive multi-wavelength coverage:</p>
        
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Optical:</strong> Pan-STARRS, SDSS, and other surveys for stellar distribution</li>
            <li><strong>Infrared:</strong> Spitzer, WISE, and Herschel data for dust and stellar mass</li>
            <li><strong>UV:</strong> GALEX for recent star formation</li>
            <li><strong>X-ray:</strong> Chandra and XMM for hot gas and X-ray binaries</li>
            <li><strong>Radio continuum:</strong> Synchrotron emission and thermal emission</li>
        </ul>
    </section>
